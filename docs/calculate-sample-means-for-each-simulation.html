6.1 Large Samples

When dealing with large samples (n > 30), we can leverage the Central Limit Theorem (CLT). This theorem states that the sampling distribution of the sample mean (average) approaches a normal distribution regardless of the population distribution (under certain conditions).

6.1.1 Example: Testing Hypothesis about Population Mean

  • Scenario: We want to test if the average height of adults in a country is 170 cm. We randomly sample 1,000 adults and measure their heights.
